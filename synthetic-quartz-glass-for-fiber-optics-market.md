<p><p>Synthetic quartz glass for fiber optics serves as a crucial material in telecommunications due to its exceptional optical properties and high resistance to temperature. The market primarily includes two production methods: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and plasma deposition. CVD involves the chemical reaction of gases to form a solid material on substrates, producing high-purity glass with uniform properties. Plasma deposition uses ionized gas to deposit layers of material, offering precise control over thickness and composition, making both methods vital for advanced optical applications.</p></p>
