<p><p>The coated endotracheal tube market is categorized primarily into orotracheal and nasotracheal intubation types. Orotracheal intubation involves inserting the tube through the mouth into the trachea, commonly used in emergency situations due to its efficiency and ease of access. In contrast, nasotracheal intubation passes the tube through the nasal passage, suited for patients who require long-term ventilation or those with facial injuries. Both methods enhance airway management while reducing the risk of trauma and infection through specialized coatings.</p></p>
