<p><ul><li>Functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging (FMRI)</li><li>Electroencephalography (EEG)</li><li>Eye Tracking</li><li>Positron Emission Tomography (PET)</li><li>Magnetoencephalography (MEG)</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Neuromarketing technology encompasses various methods to analyze consumer behavior through brain activity. Functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging (fMRI) measures brain blood flow to identify cognitive responses to marketing stimuli. Electroencephalography (EEG) monitors electrical activity in the brain, providing insights into emotional reactions. Eye tracking assesses visual attention, revealing how consumers engage with advertisements. Positron Emission Tomography (PET) observes metabolic processes in the brain, while Magnetoencephalography (MEG) records magnetic fields produced by neural activity. Other techniques may include facial coding and biometrics.</p></p>
